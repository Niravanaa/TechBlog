Exploring "Apprenticeship Patterns"

I've completed reading chapter 2 of this month's book, "Apprenticeship Patterns" by Dave Hoover and Adewale Oshineye. This chapter sheds light on personal patterns, emphasizing the importance of maintaining motivation and asking questions to broaden your knowledge in the industry.

One key takeaway is that it's crucial to stay passionate about technology and continue learning, even after securing a full-time job in the industry. Keeping your curiosity alive can lead to more significant opportunities and skill development. Another essential pattern is the willingness to admit when you don't know something and actively seek solutions within your team.

Desktop Application Development

This week, I decided to dive into desktop application development using JavaFX. I developed a desktop application that can SSH into my university's UNIX server and perform CRUD operations on web development files using a plain-text editor. The project included a file explorer, authentication fields, and SSH connections using Java Secure Channel (JSch). While it resembles an FTP client, I had a lot of fun with this project and plan to make improvements in the coming weeks.
